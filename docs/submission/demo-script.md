# Demo script

Target length: 2 minutes 50 seconds. Hard limit: under 3 minutes. Record in English with spoken narration. Replace the working project name, URL, and final evidence references before recording.

## 0:00-0:16: Why this exists

**Screen:** Home page, then a quick scroll through music, licensing, and learning.

**Voiceover:**

> I am a musician. I built Artist-Owned Platform so another musician can own the permanent digital home of their work, audience relationships, direct sales, licensing, memberships, and teaching, without making another platform the center of all of it.

## 0:16-0:42: Start with Codex

**Screen:** Codex beside `SETUP.md`; show the prompt “Help me set up my artist-owned site,” the structured proposal, and the previewed diff.

**Voiceover:**

> An artist begins by opening the repository with Codex. Codex asks about identity, audience, design, music, commerce, licensing, education, video, privacy, and deployment. It prepares a structured proposal and exact diff. The artist approves identity, rights, prices, accounts, and every consequential external action before deterministic scripts change anything.

## 0:42-1:05: Apply and reveal the site

**Screen:** Show the approval field, run the apply/check sequence, then reveal the changed fictional artist home and music pages on desktop and mobile.

**Voiceover:**

> After approval, the same setup code applies the identity and media, processes the audio, verifies the result, and records project state. The public site is a normal artist experience. It does not need an OpenAI API key and it does not place AI between the artist and the audience.

## 1:05-1:28: Music and artist ownership

**Screen:** Play a preview, open a release and track, favorite it, and show a private playlist or history.

**Voiceover:**

> The artist can publish albums, tracks, collections, credits, artwork, and previews in deliberate order. Listeners can keep favorites, playlists, and history in the artist's own Supabase project. Original source audio stays private and immutable while a durable worker creates versioned previews and waveforms.

## 1:28-1:57: Transaction, entitlement, and license

**Screen:** Choose a supported license, show exact terms and price, complete the clearly labeled local or approved Stripe test checkout, then open the account and retrieve the issued PDF.

**Voiceover:**

> Here is the authority spine. The buyer sees the exact artist-authored terms before checkout. A verified payment fact creates one order, one immutable issued license, one protected document, and the correct entitlement. Replaying the event does not duplicate access, and another customer receives a denial. Unusual uses return to the artist as an inquiry.

## 1:57-2:17: Teaching and administration

**Screen:** Resume the protected lesson, then cut to the owner administration overview and one draft/publication screen.

**Voiceover:**

> The same entitlement system protects downloads, memberships, and learning. The artist can build ordered lessons with prose, images, audio, video, and progress, then manage the whole site through an authenticated administration workspace. Drafts stay private until explicit publication.

## 2:17-2:42: What Codex built

**Screen:** Repository tree, architecture diagram, `AGENTS.md`, setup lifecycle, capability matrix, tests passing, and Git history.

**Voiceover:**

> I directed the product and supplied the operating knowledge. Codex, using GPT-5.6 Sol and Pro, wrote this new competition repository: the Nuxt application, database migrations and policies, Stripe-shaped fulfillment, workers, setup system, exports, recovery tools, tests, and documentation. A clean clone reaches the complete fictional demonstration with two commands.

## 2:42-2:55: Close

**Screen:** Home page, mobile music page, then the repository title and judging URL.

**Voiceover:**

> The personalized site is the proof. The open, agent-readable foundation underneath it is the gift: a practical way for artists to own more of the systems that make their work possible.

# a-op

**a-op: artist-owned platform** is an open-source web application for musicians.

Music leads the product. Artists publish, stream, license, and deliver their work through their own site. They control their catalog, presentation, access, customer relationships, and artist-specific source changes.

The same application supports memberships, subscriptions, Courses, video, What's New, contact, telemetry, legal-document starters, customer accounts, and administration. Every active capability joins the same artist, catalog, account, access, and administration system.

## A complete starting point

Every fresh installation begins as a composed, working site with the Sound for Movement-derived visual foundation rebuilt for React and Sites. It uses Lato, open layouts, complete light and dark themes, responsive behavior, established controls, and plain product language.

The neutral installation contains no artist content or temporary imagery. After it works as a private Site, the artist adds their own music, writing, artwork, photography, video, collaborators, course material, access plans, licensing terms, and legal language.

Music publishing, streaming, identity, access, and administration form the core. Artists activate customer libraries, downloads, licensing, memberships, subscriptions, Courses, video, What's New, contact, and telemetry as their work grows. Deactivation preserves durable records and access history.

## Connected capabilities

- Releases, albums, tracks, collections, credits, artwork, audio, streaming, queue, playlists, favorites, and listening history.
- Protected downloads, customer libraries, access grants, delivery history, and auditable entitlements.
- Artist-defined licensing options, versioned terms, inquiries, approvals, issued licenses, protected documents, credits, and customer history.
- Memberships and subscriptions with recurring access, benefits, renewals, cancellations, download credits, and license credits.
- Customer accounts for music, access, memberships, subscriptions, licenses, downloads, Courses, and activity history.
- Courses, lessons, mixed media, progress, resume, video, transcripts, structured pages, and editorial publishing.
- What's New, contact and inquiry management, first-party telemetry, Privacy Policy and Terms and Conditions starters, operations, export, and recovery.

## Artist ownership

Each artist operates their own fork and controls its deployment, content, data, configuration, customers, and publication. Artists retain ownership of their music, images, writing, video, course material, business data, and artist-authored code and source changes.

The shared application is free to start and available under `AGPL-3.0-or-later`. The license applies to the software. It does not take ownership of an artist's work or business data.

ChatGPT Work and Codex help the artist initialize, personalize, maintain, diagnose, and change their fork through natural language. The public Site, customer experience, and administration remain complete web software. Ordinary Site operation makes no model request.

## Data and access

Sites-provided D1 stores structured product state. Sites-provided R2 stores approved music, images, video, documents, and exports. Material enters ChatGPT Work only when the artist deliberately shares it there.

Server-owned roles and the central access contract protect every private stream, download, lesson, license file, and customer resource. Memberships, subscriptions, licenses, credits, and artist-issued grants create durable access that remains visible in customer and administration history.

## Commerce in Sites

The Sites installation demonstrates the complete commerce domain through Stripe Test Mode. It accepts no real payment and moves no money. Stripe hosts test payment entry, signed test events exercise orders and fulfillment, and live commerce remains unavailable in Sites.

`docs/architecture/commerce-environment.md` defines the technical boundary. A future compatible deployment requires a fresh platform review, deliberate configuration, validation, and artist approval before activating real transactions.

## Completion

A capability is complete when its public or administrative workflow runs end to end, stores the intended durable state, enforces access on the server, works across themes and responsive layouts, survives the production build, and passes a focused human-observable journey.
